Destinations in Belém

Some recommendations for destinations in Belém

Basílica de Nossa Senhora de Nazaré

The Basilica of Our Lady of Nazareth of Exile is a minor basilica of the Catholic Church located in the city of Belém. Its construction started in 1909 at the place where the image of the Virgin of Nazareth was found by Plácido José de Souza in the state of Pará, on the banks of the Murututu Igarapé.

Address: Av. Nª Sra. de Nazaré, 1300 - Nazaré.
Google Maps Link

Mercado Ver-o-Peso

The Ver-o-Peso Market, Mercado Municipal Bolonha de Peixe, Mercado de Ferro, or Ver-o-Peso is a street market and fair, and small port area inaugurated in 1901 that is part of the Ver-o-Peso Complex.

Address: Blvd. Castilhos França - Campina.
Google Maps Link

Praça da República

Leafy space with a monument to a coup of 1889, ornate colonial-era buildings & a Sunday craft fair.

Address: Av. Pres. Vargas, 814 - Campina.
Google Maps Link

Forte do Presépio

It was built in 1616 by Francisco Caldeira Castelo Branco at Maúri Point, a promontory on the right bank of the mouth of the Guamá River and Guajará Bay. The first chapel in Belém was located in the fort, now housing a museum on native indigenous culture.

Address: Praça Dom Frei Caetano Brandão, Cidade Velha.
Google Maps Link

Espaço Cultural Casa das Onze Janelas

The House of Eleven Windows, located in Belém, is a historic building that has served various purposes since its construction in the 18th century. Originally a private residence, it later became a military hospital, barracks, and a detention center during Brazil’s military dictatorship. Today, it is a cultural space and museum dedicated to modern and contemporary art, symbolizing the transformation of history through art and memory.

Address: R. Siqueira Mendes - Cidade Velha.
